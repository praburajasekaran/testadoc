5 IELTS vocabulary strategies that actually work (not the boring stuff)
Sent: 10 days after quiz completion

Hi [FIRST_NAME],

Most IELTS students waste hours memorizing vocabulary lists that never stick. Today, I'm sharing 5 strategies that actually work for building IELTS vocabulary that you'll remember and use correctly.

Strategy 1: The Context Method

Instead of memorizing isolated words, learn them in phrases and sentences. For example, don't just learn "significant" - learn "significant improvement," "significant impact," "significantly better."

Strategy 2: The Topic Clustering Method

Group vocabulary by IELTS topics (education, environment, technology, etc.). This mirrors how the test is structured and helps you recall words during the exam.

Strategy 3: The Synonym Ladder

For each word you learn, find 3-4 synonyms of varying formality levels. This is crucial for Writing Task 2 and Speaking Part 3.

Strategy 4: The Collocation Method

Learn which words naturally go together. "Make a decision" not "do a decision." "Take responsibility" not "get responsibility."

Strategy 5: The Active Usage Method

Use new vocabulary in your own sentences within 24 hours of learning it. This moves words from passive to active vocabulary.
